# Document Name: [Coding/Content] (A)

## Aliases / Search Terms

- (A)
- imaginary animal
- fictional whole animal

## Core Definition

`(A)` is coded when the content is a whole animal, but fantastic, mythic, or clearly invented.  
The form is still a whole animal, even though the referent does not exist in ordinary reality.

## Application Conditions

- The response must describe a whole animal rather than only a part.
- The fantastic quality may come from mythology, fairy tales, cartoons, or the person's own invention.
- Typical examples are a dragon, unicorn, or monster with a whole animal form.
- If the referent is a realistic whole animal, first consider [`A`](ref://scoring-input/contents/A).
- If only part of a fantastic animal is described, first consider [`(Ad)`](ref://scoring-input/contents/%28Ad%29).

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `(A)` and [`A`](ref://scoring-input/contents/A) are separated by reality versus fantasy, not by how complete the figure is.
- `(A)` and [`(Ad)`](ref://scoring-input/contents/%28Ad%29) are separated by whole versus partial animal form.
- Content does not replace the later decision about determinant, `FQ`, or `Popular`.
