# Document Name: [Coding/Content] Bl

## Aliases / Search Terms

- Bl
- blood
- bleeding

## Core Definition

`Bl` is coded when the main content is blood.  
It does not refer to a full anatomical structure; it refers to blood itself or the idea of bleeding.

## Application Conditions

- Use it when the person names blood, a stream of blood, a blood stain, or bleeding.
- It may be coded whether the blood is understood as human or animal.
- If the response mainly centers on bones, organs, or body structure, first reconsider [`An`](ref://scoring-input/contents/An).
- If the blood is part of a human or animal figure, `Bl` may be combined with [`H`](ref://scoring-input/contents/H) or [`A`](ref://scoring-input/contents/A) when appropriate.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `Bl` is not a color code; it does not replace chromatic determinant coding.
- `Bl` and [`An`](ref://scoring-input/contents/An) may both sound biological, but `Bl` centers on blood and `An` on anatomy.
- It is not enough that the blot looks red. The person has to name blood or a clearly equivalent idea.
