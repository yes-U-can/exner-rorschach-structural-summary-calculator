# Document Name: [Coding/Content] Ad

## Aliases / Search Terms

- Ad
- animal detail
- partial animal

## Core Definition

`Ad` is coded when the response names a part or detail of an animal rather than the whole animal.  
It covers a tail, head, wing, claw, or other clearly partial animal feature.

## Application Conditions

- The focus of the response must be on a part of the animal, not on the whole figure.
- The detail may be external, such as a paw or head, as long as it stays a partial animal feature.
- If the figure is a whole realistic animal, first consider [`A`](ref://scoring-input/contents/A).
- If the detail belongs to a fantastic or imaginary animal, first consider [`(Ad)`](ref://scoring-input/contents/%28Ad%29).
- If the emphasis is on anatomy or internal structure, first consider [`An`](ref://scoring-input/contents/An).

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `Ad` and [`A`](ref://scoring-input/contents/A) are separated by part versus whole.
- `Ad` and [`(Ad)`](ref://scoring-input/contents/%28Ad%29) are separated by reality versus fantasy.
- `Ad` and [`Hd`](ref://scoring-input/contents/Hd) are both detail codes, but one is animal and the other is human.
- Not every biological fragment is `Ad`; if the idea is strongly anatomical, [`An`](ref://scoring-input/contents/An) may be the better fit.
