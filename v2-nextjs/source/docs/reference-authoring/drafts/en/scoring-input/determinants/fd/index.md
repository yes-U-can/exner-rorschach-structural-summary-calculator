# Document Name: [Coding/Determinants] FD

## Aliases / Search Terms

- FD
- form-dimension
- form-based depth

## Core Definition

`FD` is coded when the impression of depth, distance, or dimension comes from form and contour rather than shading.  
The person organizes the third dimension from relative size, position, or formal arrangement.

## Application Conditions

- The dimensional quality must come from formal relations of size, edge, distance, or contour.
- The response should not depend on shading to produce depth.
- Inquiry must confirm that the idea of relief, distance, or perspective comes from form.
- If the dimension depends on shading, review [`FV`](ref://scoring-input/determinants/FV), [`VF`](ref://scoring-input/determinants/VF), or [`V`](ref://scoring-input/determinants/V).

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `FD` is not the same as [`F`](ref://scoring-input/determinants/F); `FD` adds dimensional organization.
- `FD` is not the same as [`FV`](ref://scoring-input/determinants/FV) or [`V`](ref://scoring-input/determinants/V); those responses do depend on shading.
- `FD` is also not the same as content [`Fd`](ref://scoring-input/contents/Fd); one is a determinant and the other is content.
- Phrases like "farther away," "closer," "coming out," or "behind" are not enough by themselves if they are not supported by form or contour.
