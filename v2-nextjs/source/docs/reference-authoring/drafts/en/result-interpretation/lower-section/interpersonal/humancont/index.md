# Document Name: [Interpretation/Interpersonal] Human Content

## Aliases / Search Terms

- HumanCont
- Human Content
- total human content

## Core Definition

`HumanCont` summarizes how much human content appears in the record when `H + (H) + Hd + (Hd)` are added together.
At the interpersonal level, it helps estimate how much weight people and human themes carry in the person's relational world.

## Interpretation Points

- If `HumanCont` is high, there is usually more attention to people, human scenes, or interpersonal exchange.
- If it is low, there may be less interest in human material, more relational distance, or a focus on nonpersonal objects and features.
- The variable shows amount of human representation, not its quality.
- To judge whether those representations are more integrated or more distorted, it is useful to review `PureH`, `GHR`, and `PHR`.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `HumanCont` does not include `Hx`.
- A high value does not automatically mean healthy or empathic relationships.
- A low value alone is not enough to conclude chronic isolation.

## Limits of Isolated Interpretation

- This variable reflects the weight of human themes, not the full form of relatedness.
- It should be read with `PureH`, `GHR`, `PHR`, and `H ratio`.

## Variables to Review Together

- `PureH`
- `GHR`
