# Document Name: [Interpretation/Interpersonal] AG

## Aliases / Search Terms

- AG
- aggressive movement
- aggressive imagery

## Core Definition

`AG` shows how often the record represents interpersonal scenes in aggressive, hostile, or confrontational terms.
In interpretation, it is used to read whether relatedness is more easily organized around conflict or threat.

## Interpretation Points

- If `AG` is high, there may be a greater tendency to imagine interpersonal contact as tense, aggressive, or confrontational.
- The absence of `AG` does not remove all conflict, but it lowers the weight of openly aggressive scenes.
- `AG` describes a way of representing relatedness, not direct proof of violent behavior.
- Reading it with `COP` helps distinguish predominantly cooperative records from more ambivalent or conflictual ones.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `AG` should not be translated immediately into behavioral risk.
- Its meaning changes if it appears with `MOR`, `S_aff`, or `PHR`.
- It is important to review whether aggression appears more as defense, expectation, or general imagined tone.

## Limits of Isolated Interpretation

- `AG` is only one piece of interpersonal style.
- It should be read with `COP`, `ISO_Index`, `SumT_inter`, `MOR`, and `PHR`.
