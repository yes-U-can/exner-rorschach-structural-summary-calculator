# Document Name: [Interpretation/Core] SumY

## Aliases / Search Terms

- SumY
- diffuse shading total
- total diffuse shading

## Core Definition

`SumY` is the sum of diffuse shading determinants.
In `core`, it usually reflects vague distress, difficult-to-modulate tension, or a sense of burden that feels diffuse and less controllable.

## Interpretation Points

- High `SumY` can suggest greater diffuse anxiety, emotional pressure, or a sense of overload.
- `SumY` increases the shading side of `eb` and can raise `es`.
- It is usually associated more with reactive or situational distress than with a complete structural formulation.
- Its meaning becomes clearer when compared with `m`, `D`, and `AdjEs`.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `SumY` does not by itself prove trauma, hopelessness, or a specific diagnosis.
- `SumY = 0` does not rule out distress, because other shading variables or `m` may be carrying the burden.
- It is best kept as a state-oriented indicator rather than a fixed trait label.
