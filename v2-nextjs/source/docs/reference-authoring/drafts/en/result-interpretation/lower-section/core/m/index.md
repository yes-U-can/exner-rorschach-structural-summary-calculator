# Document Name: [Interpretation/Core] m

## Aliases / Search Terms

- m
- inanimate movement total
- total inanimate movement

## Core Definition

`m` is the total number of inanimate movement responses.
In `core`, it is usually read as an indicator of reactive tension, feeling pushed by external forces, or difficult-to-control situational worry.

## Interpretation Points

- `m = 0` suggests that this kind of reactive tension is not strongly represented in the protocol.
- High `m` can be associated with pressure, helplessness, intrusive worry, or heavy situational load.
- `m` usually carries a more reactive and less integrated tone than `FM`.
- `m` weighs into `AdjEs` and helps explain why `D` can drop under recent stress.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `m` does not by itself mean total passivity or a specific disorder.
- The comparison between `D` and `AdjD` is important for separating situational strain from a more sustained pattern.
- `m` should be read with `Y` and `es`, not as an isolated value.
