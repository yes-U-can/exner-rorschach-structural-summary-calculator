# Document Name: [Interpretation/Ideation] Sum6

## Aliases / Search Terms

- Sum6
- sum of six special scores
- six special scores total

## Core Definition

`Sum6` is the simple total of responses counted within the six thought-related special scores.
It is used to estimate how often cognitive oddities, verbal deviations, or problematic ideational combinations appear in the protocol.

## Interpretation Points

- High `Sum6` suggests a greater frequency of cognitive peculiarities within the protocol.
- It is a quantity index, not a severity judgment by itself.
- In adult protocols, a clearly elevated value calls for closer review of thought organization.
- Its meaning becomes more precise when compared with `Lv2` and `WSum6`.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- If `R` is high, part of the increase may simply reflect more chances to accumulate special scores.
- High `Sum6` with mostly level-1 responses does not carry the same weight as a profile with multiple level-2 responses.
- If `Lv2`, `CONTAM`, or `M-` are also present, interpretation should become more cautious.
