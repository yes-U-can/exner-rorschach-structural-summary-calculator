# Document Name: [Interpretation/Core] SumV

## Aliases / Search Terms

- SumV
- vista total
- total vista

## Core Definition

`SumV` is the sum of vista determinants.
In `core`, it usually indicates how much self-criticism, dissatisfaction with the self, or self-evaluative discomfort is contributing to current strain.

## Interpretation Points

- High `SumV` can suggest greater self-criticism, regret, or dissatisfaction with oneself.
- In `core`, it is read as part of current burden; in `selfPerception`, it is examined more specifically as part of self-concept.
- `SumV` can contribute to `es` and reduce adaptive margin when it combines with other burden indicators.
- Its meaning becomes clearer when reviewed with other indicators of self-evaluation and dysphoria.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `SumV` does not by itself mean self-hatred or a fixed personality trait.
- A value of `0` does not rule out self-criticism if it is expressed in other ways.
- It is important to separate the current-burden reading in `core` from the broader structural reading in `selfPerception`.
