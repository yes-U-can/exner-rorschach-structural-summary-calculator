# Document Name: [Interpretation/Interpersonal] COP

## Aliases / Search Terms

- COP
- cooperative movement
- cooperative imagery

## Core Definition

`COP` shows how often the record includes cooperative or mutually coordinated interpersonal scenes.
In interpretation, it works as a clue to how easily relatedness is imagined in terms of support, coordination, or reciprocity.

## Interpretation Points

- If `COP` appears, there is often more expectation of cooperative exchange or more readiness to imagine supportive relationships.
- If `COP` is very low or absent, the record may lean less toward cooperative scenes.
- The value does not describe perfect social skill; it describes the kind of relational organization imagined most easily.
- Comparing it with `AG` helps show whether the dominant interpersonal tone is cooperative, conflictual, or mixed.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `COP` does not guarantee healthy or stable real relationships.
- Low `COP` does not automatically mean pathology.
- `COP` and `AG` can coexist in the same protocol.

## Limits of Isolated Interpretation

- `COP` offers only one sign about imagined relational structure.
- It should be read with `SumT_inter`, `HumanCont`, `ISO_Index`, and `GHR/PHR`.
