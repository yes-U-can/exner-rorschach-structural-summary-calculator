# Document Name: [Interpretation/Self Perception] H Ratio
## Aliases / Search Terms
- H ratio
- H:(H)+Hd+(Hd)
- human image ratio

## Core Definition
`H_ratio` compares whole human content with partial or imagined human content.
In `Self Perception`, it helps show whether the image of self and others rests more on whole, realistic human figures or on partial, imagined, or less integrated figures.
## Interpretation Points
- If `H` carries more weight, human representation may rest on a more complete and better integrated base.
- If `(H)`, `Hd`, or `(Hd)` dominate, the human image may feel more partial, idealized, or fragmented.
- Read together with `HumanCont` and `PureH`, it helps estimate the quality of general human representation.
- If `PHR` is high and `H_ratio` is unfavorable, human representations may look more tense or damaged.
## Cautions / Distinctions
- If total human content is very low, the ratio becomes less stable.
- `Hx` is not formally part of the ratio, although it still matters as context.
- It should be read cautiously in artistic, highly symbolic, or culture-shaped responses.
## Limits of Isolated Interpretation
- `H_ratio` does not define self-esteem or mood by itself.
- It should be read together with `MOR_self`, `HumanCont`, `GHR`, and `PHR`.
