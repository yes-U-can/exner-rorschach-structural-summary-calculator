# Document Name: [Interpretation/Ideation] WSum6

## Aliases / Search Terms

- WSum6
- Weighted Sum6
- weighted six special scores

## Core Definition

`WSum6` is the weighted sum of the six thought-related special scores.
It not only counts how many peculiarities appear, but also how much clinical weight they carry according to severity.

## Interpretation Points

- The higher `WSum6` is, the greater the overall weight of cognitive distortion or peculiarity.
- Two protocols with similar `Sum6` can still differ if one contains more severe indicators.
- In adult protocols, a high `WSum6` deserves closer review of thought consistency and disorganization risk.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `WSum6` does not replace checking which specific special scores are present.
- It is useful to see whether the increase comes from `Lv2`, `CONTAM`, or several smaller indicators accumulating.
- It should be read with `M-`, `PTI`, mediation, and processing rather than as an isolated marker.
