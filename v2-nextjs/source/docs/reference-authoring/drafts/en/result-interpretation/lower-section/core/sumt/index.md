# Document Name: [Interpretation/Core] SumT

## Aliases / Search Terms

- SumT
- texture total
- total texture

## Core Definition

`SumT` is the sum of texture determinants.
In `core`, it usually indicates how much the need for closeness, comfort, or protective contact is contributing to the current burden.

## Interpretation Points

- High `SumT` can suggest that the need for emotional proximity or support is active as part of the current load.
- `SumT` belongs to the shading side of `eb` and can increase `es`.
- In `core`, it is read first as a need or burden variable; the more relational form of that need is better explored in the interpersonal domain.
- Its meaning changes with the larger context of `eb`, `es`, and available resources.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `SumT` does not automatically mean relational warmth or good attachment capacity.
- `SumT = 0` does not prove total absence of attachment or closeness needs.
- It is useful to separate the current-burden reading in `core` from the relational-style reading in `interpersonal`.
