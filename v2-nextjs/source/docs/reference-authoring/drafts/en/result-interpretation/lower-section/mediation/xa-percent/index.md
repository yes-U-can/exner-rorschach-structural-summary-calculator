# Document Name: [Interpretation/Mediation] XA%

## Aliases / Search Terms

- XA%
- Form Appropriate Extended

## Core Definition

`XA%` indicates the overall proportion of responses whose form falls within the extended acceptable range.
Because it includes `FQ+`, `FQo`, and `FQu`, it offers a broad view of perceptual mediation.

## Interpretation Points

- If `XA%` is high, perception usually remains closer to readings that are still acceptable or shareable.
- If it drops, the probability of distortion, imprecision, or perceptual mismatch increases.
- Comparing it with `WDA%` helps show whether difficulty is broad or more concentrated in complex situations.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `XA%` does not mean complete absence of perceptual oddness; `Xu%` can still be important.
- Low `XA%` should be read together with `X-%`, not by itself.
- With few responses, the ratio can fluctuate more.
