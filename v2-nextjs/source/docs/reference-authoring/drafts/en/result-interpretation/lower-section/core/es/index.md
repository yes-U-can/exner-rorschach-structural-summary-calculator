# Document Name: [Interpretation/Core] es

## Aliases / Search Terms

- es
- Experienced Stimulations
- current demand load

## Core Definition

`es` is the sum of the components that make up `eb`.
It represents the total magnitude of current demand or internal burden being registered in the protocol.

## Interpretation Points

- High `es` suggests many active internal demands and a higher tension load.
- `es` in a moderate range suggests that current demand is not clearly overwhelming the system.
- Low `es` does not automatically mean absence of distress; it can also reflect minimization or defensive reduction.
- Its comparison with `EA` is central for reading `D` and `AdjD`.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `es` indicates burden, but it does not by itself determine whether that burden is temporary or chronic.
- Low `es` with low `EA` may speak more to general scarcity than to calmness.
- `eb` should be reviewed to see what kind of demand makes up the total.
