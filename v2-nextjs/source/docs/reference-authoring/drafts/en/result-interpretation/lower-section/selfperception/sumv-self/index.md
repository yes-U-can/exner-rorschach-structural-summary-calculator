# Document Name: [Interpretation/Self Perception] SumV

## Aliases / Search Terms

- SumV
- self perception SumV
- vista total
- self-criticism and self-observation

## Core Definition

`SumV` in `Self Perception` helps read how much critical self-observation, painful self-awareness, or review of personal limitations enters the self-image.
In `Core`, it also relates to overall burden, but here the emphasis is on self-evaluation.

## Interpretation Points

- If `SumV` is high, self-observation may lean toward guilt, shame, defect, or a sense of insufficiency.
- The variable can show that the person views the self harshly or with marked sensitivity to personal flaws.
- If `FD` also rises, self-observation may become more conscious and sustained.
- If it appears with `MOR_self`, self-image may feel more damaged or impoverished.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `SumV > 0` does not by itself prove disorder or depression.
- The variable can rise reactively during loss, frustration, or stress.
- A value of zero does not mean total absence of self-criticism.

## Limits of Isolated Interpretation

- `SumV` should be read with `FD`, `Fr+rF`, `MOR_self`, and `AdjD`.
- Its meaning changes if the record shows very little general introspection.
