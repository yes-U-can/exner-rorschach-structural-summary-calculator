# Document Name: [Interpretation/Interpersonal] PER

## Aliases / Search Terms

- PER
- personalized answers
- personal frame

## Core Definition

`PER` shows how much the person tends to justify the response from personal criteria, personal experience, or a private point of view when that style is read at the interpersonal level.
In interpretation, it suggests how much a self-referential frame shapes the person's reading of other people and relational exchange.

## Interpretation Points

- If `PER` is high, there may be greater tendency to interpret the interpersonal world through personal references rather than shared cues.
- This variable can accompany rigidity, need for self-assertion, or a very private way of validating experience.
- When it appears together with high `Xu%`, interpersonal reading may become even more idiosyncratic.
- A low value only means this bias appears less often; it does not automatically mean the person is open or flexible.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `PER` is not the same as creativity, and it does not by itself equal narcissism.
- The variable does not measure simple personal opinion; it points to the way the response is supported by a personal frame.
- It should be distinguished from the coding score `PER`, even though the two are related.

## Limits of Isolated Interpretation

- `PER` is not enough by itself to conclude relational egocentricity.
- It should be read with `a:p`, `HumanCont`, `Xu%`, and `H ratio`.

## Variables to Review Together

- `a:p`
- `HumanCont`
- `Xu%`
