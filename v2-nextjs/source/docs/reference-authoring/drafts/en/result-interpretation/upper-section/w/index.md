# Document Name: [Interpretation/Upper Section] W

## Aliases / Search Terms

- W
- whole responses
- whole-card responses

## Core Definition

`W` is the frequency of responses given to the whole blot.
In interpretation it shows how much the person tries to take in the stimulus field as a whole and whether that broad focus seems useful or not.

## Interpretation Points

- An adequate `W` may go with the ability to take in broader context.
- A high `W` may suggest a tendency to begin with the whole or to work in larger units.
- A low `W` may suggest greater emphasis on details than on the total configuration.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- High `W` does not automatically mean efficient synthesis; `DQ`, `FQ`, `W:M`, and `Zd` still matter.
- Low `W` does not by itself equal narrowness; it may coexist with a practical, detail-oriented style.
- If `W` is high while `Dd` is also high, broad scanning may coexist with dispersion.
