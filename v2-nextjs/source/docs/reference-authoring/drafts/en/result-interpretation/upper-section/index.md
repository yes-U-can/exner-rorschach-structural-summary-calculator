# Document Name: [Interpretation/Upper Section] Upper Section

## Aliases / Search Terms

- upper-section
- upper section
- upper section of structural summary

## Core Definition

`Upper Section` is the part of the structural summary where `Zf`, `ZSum`, `ZEst`, `Zd`, `W`, `D`, `Dd`, `S`, and the `DQ` distribution are reviewed first.
It works as an initial screen for how the person organizes the stimulus field, where attention is placed, and how well responses are generally structured.

## Interpretation Points

- The `Z` family helps estimate frequency, energy, and efficiency of organizational effort.
- `W`, `D`, `Dd`, and `S` show whether attention is directed toward wholes, common details, unusual details, or white space.
- The `DQ` distribution summarizes the general level of formal integration or organization in the responses.
- This block usually connects next with `processing` and `mediation`.

## Cautions / Distinctions

- `Zf` or `ZSum` alone are not enough; `Zd` usually matters as well.
- A high `W` count does not automatically mean efficient broad scanning.
- A high `Dd` or `S` count should not be read as pathology by itself.
- Low `DQ+` can also appear in brief or highly defensive protocols.
